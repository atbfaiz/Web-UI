Pay after delivery

One of the many benefits offered to PayPal customers is the Pay After Delivery service. With this benefit, payments are debited after 14 days from the customer’s bank account.

The challenge was to make the process of linking a bank account to a PayPal account easier, and to get permission to set up a Direct Debit.

The solution was to structure the messaging hierarchy in a way that’s easy to understand, and make the copy helpful and actionable. We needed to address the confusion found during research around the unfamiliarity of Direct Debit, how this particular service was available, and if purchases would arrive because there was a 14-day gap before their accounts were debited. Success was measured by the number of people who signed up to use this benefit.

My role was to write the copy while maintaining the UI of the brand to build the messaging hierarchy. I also made sure it was simple, helpful, and trustworthy. I also consulted with the Legal and Go-to-Market teams to make sure it met legal and local requirements.

Return shipping refund

As a benefit to customers, if a purchase isn’t what was expected, they can send it back, and get the shipping cost refunded by PayPal. As of early 2018, this could only be done on the website, and not in the app.

The challenge was to guide the user to the website to complete the process of requesting a refund.

The solution was to make sure the copy was instructional and guides the user to the next step. Success is measured by the number of people who continue and submit their refund.

My role was to work on the hierarchy of the messaging, write the copy, and make sure it’s simple, clear, and actionable.

As the main content designer for this benefit, I owned the content strategy end-to-end. This is one of the several projects I worked on for this benefit.
