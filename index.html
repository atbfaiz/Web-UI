Spotify


The challenge was to let people in countries (like India and Brazil) sign up for services using their phone number. This wasn’t available on Spotify.

The solution was to build a method where people could use their phone number to sign up or log in to their Spotify account. Success is measured by the numbers/percentage of people using this option to sign up.

PayPal


The challenge was to make the process of linking a bank account to a PayPal account easier, and to get permission to set up a Direct Debit.


The solution was to structure the messaging hierarchy in a way that’s easy to understand, and make the copy helpful and actionable. We needed to address the confusion found during research around the unfamiliarity of Direct Debit, how this particular service was available, and if purchases would arrive because there was a 14-day gap before their accounts were debited. Success was measured by the number of people who signed up to use this benefit.

Cinema Club


Cinema Club is a simple utility app meant to make going out to a cinema as simple as hailing an Uber.


Cinema Club started as an internal experiment at YPlan, which eventually became a standalone product, helping YPlan acquire and retain cinema bookers. We wanted to create a better experience for finding films, showtimes and booking tickets in cities like London or New York.
To be sure we're addressing the right problems we designed our process around regular cycles of prototyping and face to face user sessions, validating every step of the way with the input from real users. Our starting point was a straightforward list of films currently shown in the cinemas, organized by popularity.
